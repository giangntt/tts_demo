Vietnamese Voice Adaptation in TTS

A Modular and Data-Efficient Framework

This work presents a modular and data-efficient framework for Vietnamese voice adaptation in text-to-speech systems. Building on a compact zero-shot TTS backbone, the approach integrates a language-aware Mixture-of-Experts module together with style and prosody conditioning to improve pronunciation, naturalness, and speaker fidelity under limited data and compute. A staged fine-tuning strategy enables effective adaptation in both zero-shot and few-shot settings. Experiments show reduced word error rate and higher speaker similarity compared to Vietnamese baselines, with performance approaching models trained on much larger datasets. The results demonstrate that high-quality Vietnamese voice cloning is achievable without large-scale resources.

Zero-Shot & Few-Shot Vietnamese Voice Adaptation

The framework demonstrates effective adaptation to different Vietnamese voices, accents, and speaking styles. Each demo shows a reference prompt audio and the synthesized output for the given text, showcasing the model's ability to maintain speaker fidelity and naturalness.

Cross-Language Adaptation

The language-aware framework enables effective voice adaptation across languages. Here we demonstrate Vietnamese-to-English and English-to-Vietnamese voice adaptation, maintaining natural pronunciation and voice characteristics.

Code-Switch Adaptation

Real-world conversations often mix languages. The framework handles code-switching seamlessly, maintaining natural pronunciation and voice characteristics across language boundaries through the language-aware MoE module.
