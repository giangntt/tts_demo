Voice Adaptation for Vietnamese
Text-to-Speech in Low-Resource Settings

A Modular and Resource-Efficient Framework

This work presents a modular and resource-efficient framework for Vietnamese voice adaptation in text-to-speech systems. Building on a compact zero-shot TTS backbone, the approach integrates a language-aware Mixture-of-Experts module together with enhanced speech conditioning modules to improve intelligibility, naturalness, and speaker fidelity under limited data and compute. A staged fine-tuning strategy enables effective adaptation in both zero-shot and few-shot settings. Experiments show reduced word error rate and higher speaker similarity compared to baselines, with speaker similarity approaching the ground truth levels. The results demonstrate that high-quality Vietnamese voice cloning is achievable without large-scale resources.
